Don Revie (North) Stand (The Kop):

Known for its fervent atmosphere, The Kop is where some of the most passionate Leeds United supporters gather on matchdays. With its steep stands and close proximity to the pitch, The Kop offers fans unparalleled views of the action, allowing them to immerse themselves fully in the drama unfolding on the field. The thunderous chants and deafening roars that emanate from The Kop reverberate throughout Elland Road, energizing players and fans alike and creating an electric atmosphere that is second to none. Whether it's singing traditional anthems, waving flags, or orchestrating coordinated displays, the fans in The Kop play a vital role in driving the team forward and inspiring memorable performances. Their unwavering loyalty and unwavering support make The Kop a symbol of the enduring passion and pride that define Leeds United Football Club.

Norman Hunter South Stand:

The Norman Hunter South Stand at Elland Road is dedicated to the legendary Leeds United player and exclusively hosts home fans. This stand, named after the revered footballing icon Norman Hunter, offers a matchday experience like no other. With its modern facilities, including concession stands and amenities tailored to the needs of supporters, the South Stand ensures that fans can enjoy the game in comfort and style. The vibrant atmosphere created by the passionate home crowd further enhances the excitement of the matches, fueling the team with unwavering support and enthusiasm. As an integral part of Elland Road's identity and history, the Norman Hunter South Stand stands as a testament to the club's rich heritage and the enduring spirit of its loyal fanbase.

John Charles (West) Stand:

The John Charles West Stand at Elland Road honors the Welsh footballing legend and accommodates general admission and season ticket holders. Away fans are housed on one side of the stand, towards the South Stand, with a normal allocation of 1,566 seats in the upper tier. If required, an additional 1,104 seats can be provided in the lower tier, making the overall allocation 2,670. Offering panoramic views of the pitch, the West Stand provides modern amenities such as concession stands and accessible seating areas. With its comfortable facilities and lively atmosphere, the West Stand enhances the matchday experience for fans, contributing to the rich history and identity of Elland Road.

Jack Charlton (East) Stand:

The Jack Charlton East Stand at Elland Road is named in honor of the Leeds United and England legend, Jack Charlton. Formerly known as the Lowfields Road stand, it underwent renovations and was officially renamed to commemorate Charlton's contributions to the club's history. The East Stand provides corporate hospitality suites, executive boxes, and media facilities, catering to VIP guests and special events. It offers a premium matchday experience with modern amenities and excellent views of the pitch. The stand's spacious layout and comfortable facilities make it a popular choice for fans looking to enjoy the game in style. As an iconic part of Elland Road, the Jack Charlton East Stand adds to the stadium's rich heritage and provides a fitting tribute to one of Leeds United's greatest players.

The Billy Bremner Statue:

The Billy Bremner Statue is a bronze sculpture located outside Elland Road Stadium, home to Leeds United Football Club. It commemorates the club's legendary captain, Billy Bremner, who played a pivotal role in the team's success during the 1960s and 1970s. Unveiled in 1999, the statue stands as a lasting tribute to Bremner's contributions to Leeds United and the sport of football as a whole. Positioned near the main entrance of the stadium, the statue serves as a focal point for fans, providing a place for reflection and remembrance of one of the club's most iconic figures. Visitors to Elland Road often take photographs with the statue, paying homage to Bremner's legacy and the enduring spirit of Leeds United.

The Jack Charlton Memorial Garden:

The Jack Charlton Memorial Garden is a dedicated space located near Elland Road Stadium, serving as a tribute to the late Leeds United and England footballing legend, Jack Charlton. This serene garden provides fans and visitors with a place for reflection and remembrance of Charlton's remarkable contributions to both the club and the sport of football. Featuring floral displays, sculptures, and seating areas, the memorial garden offers a peaceful environment where fans can pay their respects and honor Charlton's enduring legacy. Positioned as a poignant reminder of Charlton's impact on Leeds United and the wider footballing community, the memorial garden stands as a fitting tribute to one of the club's most beloved figures.

The Leeds United Megastore:

The Leeds United Megastore is the official retail outlet located within Elland Road Stadium, serving as a hub for fans to purchase a wide range of club merchandise and memorabilia. Boasting an extensive selection of apparel, accessories, and souvenirs, the megastore offers supporters the opportunity to show their allegiance to the club in style. From replica jerseys and scarves to novelty items and gifts, the store caters to fans of all ages and preferences. Staffed by friendly and knowledgeable personnel, the megastore provides a welcoming shopping experience for visitors to Elland Road, whether they're attending a match or simply exploring the stadium. As a key destination for Leeds United enthusiasts, the megastore plays an essential role in connecting fans with the club's identity and heritage.

The South Stand Mural:

The South Stand Mural at Elland Road Stadium is a vibrant artwork that adorns the walls of the stand, capturing iconic moments and symbols of Leeds United's history and identity. Stretching across the interior of the South Stand, the mural serves as a visual celebration of the club's rich heritage, featuring depictions of legendary players, memorable matches, and emblematic imagery associated with Leeds United. Designed to inspire and captivate fans, the mural contributes to the electric atmosphere of matchdays, creating a sense of pride and belonging among supporters. As a prominent feature of Elland Road, the South Stand Mural serves as a powerful reminder of the club's legacy and the passion of its dedicated fanbase.
