Don Revie (North) Stand (The Kop):

The Kop at Elland Road is legendary, known far and wide for its passionate atmosphere that encapsulates the very essence of Leeds United. This iconic stand, situated close to the pitch, offers fans unparalleled views of the action, allowing them to feel intimately connected to every pass, tackle, and goal. What truly sets The Kop apart is the sea of loyal Leeds United supporters who gather here, transforming the stand into a fortress of noise and energy. From the moment the match begins, the air is filled with thunderous chants and deafening roars that reverberate throughout the stadium. These vocal supporters don't just watch the game; they live it, breathing life into the players and inspiring them to perform at their best. The Kop is more than just a section of the stadium; it’s a symbol of the club's rich history and undying spirit. The unwavering loyalty and support displayed by the fans in The Kop are central to the pride and identity of Leeds United Football Club. Every match day, this electric ambiance turns Elland Road into an intimidating venue for visiting teams and a fortress for the home side. Being part of The Kop is about more than just watching football—it's about belonging to a community of dedicated fans who share an unbreakable bond with the club. It's a place where memories are made, legends are born, and the spirit of Leeds United is celebrated with every cheer.

Norman Hunter South Stand:

The Norman Hunter South Stand at Elland Road is dedicated to the legendary Leeds United player and exclusively hosts home fans. Named after Norman Hunter, it offers a unique matchday experience with modern facilities and amenities. The passionate home crowd creates a vibrant atmosphere, fueling the team with unwavering support. As a key part of Elland Road's identity, the Norman Hunter South Stand honors the club's rich heritage and loyal fanbase.
