Don Revie (North) Stand (The Kop):

The Kop at Elland Road is legendary, known far and wide for its passionate atmosphere that encapsulates the very essence of Leeds United. This iconic stand, situated close to the pitch, offers fans unparalleled views of the action, allowing them to feel intimately connected to every pass, tackle, and goal. What truly sets The Kop apart is the sea of loyal Leeds United supporters who gather here, transforming the stand into a fortress of noise and energy. From the moment the match begins, the air is filled with thunderous chants and deafening roars that reverberate throughout the stadium. These vocal supporters don't just watch the game; they live it, breathing life into the players and inspiring them to perform at their best. The Kop is more than just a section of the stadium; it’s a symbol of the club's rich history and undying spirit. The unwavering loyalty and support displayed by the fans in The Kop are central to the pride and identity of Leeds United Football Club. Every match day, this electric ambiance turns Elland Road into an intimidating venue for visiting teams and a fortress for the home side. Being part of The Kop is about more than just watching football—it's about belonging to a community of dedicated fans who share an unbreakable bond with the club. It's a place where memories are made, legends are born, and the spirit of Leeds United is celebrated with every cheer.

Norman Hunter South Stand:

The Norman Hunter South Stand at Elland Road is dedicated to the legendary Leeds United player, Norman Hunter, and exclusively hosts home fans. Named in honor of Hunter's immense contribution to the club, this stand offers a unique matchday experience with modern facilities and amenities that enhance the overall atmosphere. Home to some of the most passionate supporters, the Norman Hunter South Stand creates a vibrant and lively environment that fuels the team with unwavering support. The passionate home crowd, known for their vocal and enthusiastic backing, plays a crucial role in energizing the players and creating an intimidating atmosphere for opponents. As a key part of Elland Road's identity, the Norman Hunter South Stand not only honors the club's rich heritage but also highlights the loyalty and dedication of its fanbase. It stands as a testament to the enduring spirit of Leeds United, where the legacy of past heroes like Norman Hunter continues to inspire current and future generations.

John Charles (West) Stand:

The John Charles West Stand at Elland Road honors the Welsh footballing legend, John Charles, and accommodates both general admission and season ticket holders. This stand is strategically designed to provide a comfortable and enjoyable matchday experience for all fans. Away fans are housed on one side of the stand, towards the South Stand, with a normal allocation of 1,566 seats in the upper tier. If required, an additional 1,104 seats can be provided in the lower tier, making the overall allocation 2,670. This arrangement ensures that visiting supporters have a designated area, while maintaining the vibrant atmosphere created by the home crowd. Offering panoramic views of the pitch, the West Stand is equipped with modern amenities such as concession stands and accessible seating areas, ensuring that all fans can enjoy the game in comfort. The stand's facilities are designed to enhance the matchday experience, providing convenience and accessibility to all spectators. With its comfortable facilities and lively atmosphere, the John Charles West Stand plays a crucial role in the overall experience at Elland Road. It contributes to the rich history and identity of the stadium, celebrating the legacy of John Charles while providing a welcoming environment for both home and away supporters.
